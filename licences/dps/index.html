About the Digital Public Space Licence

The Digital Public Space Licence, version 1.0 was published in July 2013 and is intended for the licensing of non-executable copyright material (that is, content other than software programs, such as datasets, and documents). It is intended to be used by any copyright holder wishing to permissively licence their works to the public.

The Digital Public Space Licence is a public copyright licence which is derived from, and intended to be generally equivalent to, the Open Government Licence.
